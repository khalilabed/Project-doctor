10 Top Questions on Pulmonary Rehab for COPD

Dr. Linda toben

3 Jan 2023

12 Comments

99%

If you have chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) or another lung condition that makes it hard to breathe, such as asthma or interstitial lung disease, your doctor may recommend pulmonary rehabilitation (PR), a medically supervised exercise and education program.


“PR is a cornerstone of treatment for people with chronic respiratory diseases,” says Eric Lang, M.D., a pulmonologist at the University of Miami Health System in Florida. If you’re new to the term, you probably have questions about what, exactly, goes on in pulmonary rehab and how best to prepare. We got the details from top pulmonologists on what kind of equipment you’ll use, how long your sessions will last (and how many you’ll likely do), whether (or not) most insurance plans will cover PR programs, and more.


What Is Pulmonary Rehab? PR is a structured program that combines a range of medical interventions that are designed to help you breathe better. The big-picture goal? To improve your physical and psychological health. Pulmonary rehab will include an exercise program aimed at improving your breathing, but it’s much more than that: PR also involves education on your disease and how best to manage it. This part of the program may be conducted in a one-on-one setting or in a group.
